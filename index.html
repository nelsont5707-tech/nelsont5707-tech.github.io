William Blake (1757–1827) was an English poet, painter, and printmaker. During his life, most people overlooked his work, but today he’s seen as one of the most influential voices in both poetry and visual art.

Famous Works

  1. Songs of Innocence and of Experience (1794)
  2. The Marriage of Heaven and Hell (1790–93)
  3. Jerusalem: The Emanation of the Giant Albion (1804–20)
